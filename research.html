Summer 2018: Ramsey's SOF Method
I spend most of my summer research at Conover's lab studying how we could use Ramsey's oscillatory field method to make high-precision measurements without the need for AC Stark shift elimination. While this was an experimental project, there was not much experimental work involved, as we already had a functioning system for high-precision spectroscopy: (i) Rydberg excitation, (ii) DC-Stark shift elimination, (iii) AC-Stark shift elimination. To run the Ramsey experiment, I simply replaced the previous single-pulse Rydberg excitation with a double-pulse Rydberg excitation (which produced Ramsey fringes), and removed step (iii). It turned out probably 10% of the time was spent on taking data, while the rest went to deriving (from Ramsey's 1950 paper) a model for fitting the data to find the desired (with zero AC-Startk shift) transition frequency.

I presented this work at Colby Undergraduate Summer Research Retreat 2018 (CUSRR 2018). Check out my poster here.

I will reproduce the derivation for the model here later when I have time. But if you are curious, check out the handwritten version here. The end result was correct but the path I took could be misleading for unfamiliar readers, so beware!
