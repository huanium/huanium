08 Jan 2019
Lab update: I probably spent at least 3 hours untangling a not-very-well-put-together circuit. Have to say the task was quite fun but not enjoyable at times when a solder iron and pliers were the only means of solving that messy, possibly accidental, puzzle. One thing I learned from doing quite a bit of soldering recently: sometimes not having such a bright light source makes soldering easier. The problem with a lot of light is a lot of reflection. Having a less direct light source and paying attention to the silhouttes of the components can give a better "feel" to where the components are, their relative sizes, and whether there are short-circuits.
